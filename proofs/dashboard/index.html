Demographic Model
Asymmetric split: source and target diverge from common ancestor for t generations, with independent Nₑ.
Two demes at migration–drift equilibrium. M = 4Nₑm (scaled migration rate).
Divergence Parameters
Split time (generations) 500
Source Nₑ 10000
Target Nₑ 10000
Genetic drift accumulates with time and shrinks with population size. A bottlenecked target population drifts faster, losing more PGS variance than the source.
Migration Parameters
Scaled migration rate (M) 1.00
Higher gene flow (larger M) means less differentiation between demes. At equilibrium, within-deme coalescence time is always 2 units; between-deme time grows as gene flow decreases.
Trait Architecture
Heritability (h²) 0.50
Heritability sets the total additive genetic variance.
Glossary
F_ST — fraction of genetic variation due to population structure (0 = identical, 1 = fully diverged)
PGS — polygenic score: weighted sum of genetic variants predicting a trait
V_A — additive genetic variance: how much PGS varies in the ancestral population
h² — heritability: fraction of trait variation explained by genetics
Nₑ — effective population size: genetic equivalent of census size
M — scaled migration: 4Nₑm, higher = more gene flow between demes
δ — equilibrium differentiation between demes under migration-drift balance
MAF — minor allele frequency: how common the less-frequent variant is
